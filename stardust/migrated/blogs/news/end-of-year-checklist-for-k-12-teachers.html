End-of-Year Checklist for K–12 Teachers

As the school year comes to an end, it’s time to reflect, refresh, and prepare for a well-earned break. Here’s a simple checklist to help teachers wrap up the year with confidence and clarity.

1. Organize and Archive

  • Back up lesson plans, student data, and digital resources.
  • File away assessments and notes for next year’s reference.
  • Clean out your classroom or digital workspace—start fresh for fall.

2. Reflect and Celebrate

  • Take a moment to recognize your wins both big and small.
  • Gather student feedback or reflections to inspire next year’s goals.
  • Celebrate milestones with your colleagues and students.

3. Renew Your Professional Growth

  • Review your district’s recertification timeline.
  • Explore CE Credits Online courses to earn graduate-level credits for salary advancement or license renewal.
  • Choose topics that align with your classroom goals such as: SEL, trauma-informed teaching, or classroom management.

4. Recharge and Reset

  • Plan genuine rest; whether that’s travel, reading, or simply unplugging.
  • Reflect on what energized you this year and what you’d like to change.
  • Set gentle intentions for the Summer. Low pressure goals are great.

5. Plan Ahead

  • Note curriculum updates or new standards to review.
  • Create a short “first week” plan for next year to ease the transition.
  • Bookmark resources or PD courses to revisit mid-summer.

You’ve made an impact this year! Now it’s time to rest, reflect, and recharge. CE Credits Online is here to help you start next year strong with flexible, accredited courses designed for real classroom success.
